Lumiere Research Program

Effects of Argon Ion Bombardment on Amorphous Silicon and Copper Indium Gallium Diselenide Solar Cells

A simulation was conducted using SRIM/TRIM software to study the sputtering yield of Amorphous Silicon (a-Si) and Copper Indium Gallium Di-Selenide (CIGS) solar cells when exposed to argon ions. This research aimed to evaluate how ion energy, incidence angle, and material structure affect solar cell performance and durability. Findings will help optimize solar cell efficiency for various environmental conditions, offering cost-effective and energy-efficient solutions. Although the simulation highlights valuable insights, it faces limitations in studying complex environmental factors and advanced solar cell types, such as organic and quantum cells, due to technological constraints.
